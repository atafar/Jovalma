HEALTHY FOOD PYRAMID

The triangular shape of the nutritional pyramid guides us on the frequency and the quantities we have to consume from different foods. In the first links, the foods that have the most weight should be placed in the daily diet are placed. As we go up the levels, other foods that have to be consumed in smaller quantities appear.

On the vertex, consumer foods are optional and, if consumed, it should be occasionally.

FOODS AT DIFFERENT LEVELS

The nutrition pyramid, also known as the food pyramid or pyramid, is usually the method used to suggest the variety of foods to be consumed on a daily basis.

The one that proposes the pyramid is the distribution by consumption of the type or group of foods that compose it in similar propositions to the one of the layers or steps.

  • The lower steps mostly propose complex carbohydrates.
  • The central steps suggest foods that contain less carbohydrates, more proteins and fats and a greatcontent vitamin.
  • The upper, bottom-up, high levels contain amount of proteins and amount of grases.
  • The tallest strand contains mostly fat and simple carbohydrates, all foods that provide mainly calories; and for that reason they are recommended in smaller quantity.

Basic nutrition rules

  • Drink 1.5L - 2L of water daily.
  • 60 minutes of physical activity per day.
  • Eat a great variety of foods.
  • Balance the foods we consume with physical activity, to maintain and / or improve body weight.
  • Mainly ingest whole grains, fruits and vegetables.
  • Keeping a low diet in saturated glands and cholesterol.
  • Follow a moderate diet in sugars.
  • Follow a low or moderate salt diet (sodium).

The quantity or number of portions of each food that a person must consume depends on several personal factors:

  • Weight
  • The age
  • Sex
  • The physical activity

Recommended portions

  • Bread, cereals, pasta and rice: 6/11 servings per day.

1 serving is equivalent to: 1 slice of bread, 1/2 cup cooked pasta or cereal, 28 gr of ready-to-eat cereal, ½ roll, 3-4 crackers, small crackers.

  • Vegetables: 3/5 servings per day.

1 serving is equivalent to: ¾ cup of vegetable juice broth, ½ cup of cooked vegetables, 1 cup of raw vegetables, ½ cups of beans and cooked peas.

  • Fruits: 2/4 servings per day.

1 serving is equivalent to: 1 unit of medium size, 1 cut of melon, ½ cup of strawberries, 3/4 cup of juice, ½ cup of canned or dyed fruit.

  • Milk, yogurt or cheeses: 2/3 servings per day.

1 serving is equivalent to: 1 cup of milk or yogurt, 60 g of soft cheese or 50 gr of cheese.

  • Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, dried beans and nuts: 2/3 servings per day.

1 serving is equivalent to: 70 grams of grilled meat, poultry or fish (a daily total of 150-200 gr), 1 egg, ½ cup legumes cooked, 30 gr. Of nuts.

They are high fat foods: margarine, butter, mayonnaise, cream, cream cheese, sauces.

RELATED PARTIES WITH LIFE STYLE

Healthy lifestyle tips that have an impact greater than 40% on how to become ill or die in the most developed countries are as follows:

  1. Physical Activity: Daily Exercise It was already a recommendation, but now it is important to spend an hour a day. 10,000 daily steps can be a reference framework.
  2. Emotional balance: Our mood conditions our eating needs. Sadness can get us hungry and anxiety can cause us to eat compulsively. But also the emotional state is reflected when buying, choosing, cooking and consuming. That's why the concept of "conscious eating" is important or trying to put aside the mood when we talk about feeding.
  3. Energy balance: Importantly, the balance between energy intake (what we eat) and energy expenditure (what we spend on basal metabolism, thermogenesis and physical activity). In this balance factors such as the size of the ration, the frequency of consumption (it is advisable to split the total intake in 5 daily precautions) or chronobiology: the importance of the time to ingest. For example, there is evidence that the intake has a greater metabolic performance at the beginning of the day, hence the importance of breakfast.
  4. Healthy culinary techniques: how we cook will have an impact on our health. The most effective healthy cooking technique is steaming, but also boiled, grilled or baked better than fried. It is important that recipients do not transfer any element to food. And to avoid it the best is the glass. Regarding the tools to handle foods (foams, spoons ...), the most suitable ones are those made of silicone, which prevent the teflon from getting out of the pans. Wooden spoons are also advisable, but they must be washed at the dishwasher at least once a week to eliminate possible mushrooms.
  5. Drink water: Between 4 and 6 glasses a day of water, at least, and complete with other liquids until it reaches, as an average daily, approximately 2 liters.
