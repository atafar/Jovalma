WHAT ARE THE SOCIAL SKILLS?

Social skills are the set of behavioral strategies and the capabilities to apply these behaviors that help us to solve a social situation effectively, that is, acceptable to the subject and the social context in which it is.

They allow expressing feelings, attitudes, desires, opinions or rights in a manner that is appropriate to the situation in which the individual finds himself while respecting the behaviors of others. Thus, they make us improve our interpersonal relationships, feel good, get what we want and achieve that others do not prevent us from achieving our goals.

These behaviors are necessary to interact and interact with each other in an effective and mutually satisfactory way.

So, social skills are the set of behaviors that allow us to relate to each other satisfactorily, which is why they are essential in any environment that we can find (family, work, the street, etc.).

Proper implementation of these abilities is beneficial for learning to express oneself and understanding others, taking into account the needs and interests of the whole world, trying to find the most satisfying solution for all in the face of a problem or being solidarity, fundamental things if we want to live in society.

COMPLEXAL SOCIAL SKILLS:

  • Empathy. Ability to put oneself in the place of the other person.
  • Emotional intelligence.Emotional intelligence is the social ability of a person to manage feelings and emotions, discriminate among them and use these knowledge to direct their own thoughts and actions (Salovey and Mayer, 1990).
  • Asertivity.Ability to be clear, frank and direct, saying what it means, without hurting the feelings of others or despising the value of others, just by defending their rights as a person.
  • Active listening ability. Be able to listen with understanding and care, understanding what the other person means and conveying that we have received their message.
  • Ability to communicate feelings and emotions. Be able to express our feelings in front of others in a correct way, whether they are positive or negative.
  • Ability to define a problem and evaluate solutions. A person's social ability to analyze a situation taking into account the objective elements, as well as the feelings and needs of each person.
  • Negotiation Communication capability aimed at finding a solution that is satisfactory for all parties.
  • Modulation of emotional expression. Ability to adapt the expression of our emotions to the environment.
  • Ability to apologize. Ability to be aware of the errors made and recognize them.
  • Recognition and defense of their own and others' rights. Ability to be aware of our rights and those of others and defend them in a proper way.

EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE

Emotional intelligence is the ability we have to manage, understand or manage our own emotions. In turn, it not only helps to improve our quality of life, but also to develop and have better relationships with others, more respectful and comprehensive. In addition, our quality of life is directly proportional to the quality of our mental state, for this reason it is so important.

Some of the keys to emotional intelligence are the following:

  • Self Awareness: Tell us or know how to recognize our emotions and not let ourselves be cared for by them. Mental strength
  • Awareness: learn to be more aware of what is happening in our interior and accept the power of our vulnerability.
  • Self-analysis: learn to evaluate our emotions and be able to identify them; Observe where our focus is or where we are focusing our attention. In this way, we can understand from where these emotions arise.
  • Self control: the ability to not react and know how to measure your patience. Learn to take time to breathe, reflect, and take a distance from a situation. Thus we will have a better perspective and greater emotional control.
  • Empathy: Sometimes we are so embroiled in our own problems, or our ego strives to be right, that we were locked in a tunnel with a single vision. In other words: we lose the ability to understand the point of view of others or because of a situation. Without a doubt, empathy is necessary to recognize the feelings of others, eliminate indifference and increase our level of understanding.
