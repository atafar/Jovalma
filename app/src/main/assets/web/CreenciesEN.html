FALSE BELOW ABOUT TCA

Anorexia and bulimia are the only TCA

FALS. In addition to anorexia and bulimia, there are other TCAs such as tinnitus disorder, or the disorder of unspecified eating behavior (TCANE).

Anorexia is the most frequent disorder

FALS. As reflected in the Guide of Clinical Practice on Behavioral Disorders prepared by the Ministry of Health and Consumption, more cases of bulimia and disorder of unspecified eating behavior are diagnosed than anorexia.

Whenever someone suffers an ACT he is very thin

FALSE. In many cases, the physical appearance of the person suffering from an ACT is normal. That is, it is not necessarily thin or excessively thin. To mistakenly believe that everyone who suffers a TCA is thin can make it difficult to detect it.

The TCAs are not healed at all

FALSE. As reflected in the Clinical Practice Guideline on Behavioral Disorders developed by the Ministry of Health and Consumption, around 50-60% of the cases recovered completely, 20-30% do so partially, and only 10- 20% chronicles the disease. What is essential for the recovery of an ACT is the realization of one specialized medical and psychological treatment.

Someone who suffers an ACT has it because he "searched for it"

FALSE. TCAs are mental disorders that nobody chooses to suffer. They are disorders that cause severe suffering both to the affected person and to their family environment. They always have a multicausal origin in which different factors of individual, family and social interest take part, so that the final result is that there are people more or less vulnerable to suffering an ACT, never people who decide to suffer it.

TCAs are just a matter of young girls

FALS. Although ACTs are more common in women (9 out of 10 cases are women), they affect both sexes. They are also diagnosed in people of all ages. What is usual is that the age of onset of the disease is placed in adolescence, and once it has appeared, the disease accompanies the person until it is treated.

Affinities typical of bulimia and disorder disorder are a problem ofwillpower

FALS. When we talk about bulimia and disorder we talk about mental disorders that are characterized by the fact that the person affected does not have the ability to control their intake. In no way is it a lack of will.
