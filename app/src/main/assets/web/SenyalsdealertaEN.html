How can we help a person who worries us?

It is very important to be honest, direct and comprehensive. You have to sit quietly and explain exactly what you have noticed without saving any detail. You have to tell the person who really is very worried about what is happening and you should know that, as it really matters to you, you would like to help. It can be said: "It seems to me that, perhaps, you have a food disorder or problems with food." Do not accuse him, condemn him or make him confess anything; Follow your sting, but you do not want to be your therapist.

Suggest professional help. Most people respond better if they are given different options. For example, we can say: "Why do not you call the Association Against Anorexia and Bulimia?", "You could get more information in a mutual aid group" or "You could ask the dietitian for an hour." You can offer to accompany it for help.

If he / she refuses to be helped or denied the problem, it may not be a matter of this since he may not be prepared to admit that he has a problem. Do not help her to deny it with your silence. Talk about the things you observe and what you care about. You can not force him to seek help, but you can let him know where you can go or call to request information and you can even suggest that you start to get a medical examination. Reaffirm that you are willing to talk about the problem, but only if you want and at the time it deems appropriate. Do not fret over the issue of food or weight.

Friends, colleagues, and family members tend to get too involved in the problems of the affected person. It should be remembered that in eating disorders control aspects intervene and if you try to control the person, it will always win. It should not be attempted to handle bribes, rewards, punishments or guilt. None of these tactics works. The key is to make you feel our support.

Whether the person is in treatment or not, do not make the mistake of trying to change their behavior. Let him / her do it as it is the only one who can change it. If you involve too much, you can get upset and end up burning. Although it is frustrating, that is all that a friend can do. Everything you can do is not make the person cuddle or even take this responsibility yourself.
