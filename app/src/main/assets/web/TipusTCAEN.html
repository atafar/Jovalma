DISORDERS OF FOOD CONDUCT

Disorders are mental disorders characterized by a pathological behavior before the food intake and an obsession with weight control. They are disorders of multifactorial origin, originated by the interaction of different causes of biological, psychological, family and sociocultural origin. They are diseases that cause negative consequences for both the physical and mental health of the person.

TCAs are serious illnesses but can be cured if the person is treated with a team of doctors and psychologists specialized in TCA. They are long and complex treatments. A common feature of these disorders is the lack of awareness of disease on the part of the affected person. This means that the affected person is not able to identify the negative consequences of the disorder, nor the need to treat, nor the benefits of the treatment. This fact hinders adherence to treatment in some cases. However, the role of the family is especially important in trying to get the affected person to receive the treatment they need even if they do not cooperate in receiving it.


 TYPE OF TCA MORE FREQUENT

Anorexia Nervosa

People who suffer anorexia nervosa feel an intense fear of gaining weight or gain weight, and are overly concerned about their silhouette. As a consequence, they present anomalous behaviors in terms of diet, body weight and volume and body shape. The person may look fat or disproportionate, despite having a weight below what is considered normal.

Suffering this disorder has a great negative impact on family, social, work, academic and emotional life. At the beginning of the disease it is possible that excessive problems are not observed. It is even possible that those affected feel satisfied with the weight loss, and are often rewarded by their environment. But after a while the symptoms appear that indicate that something is not working. We must be alert to certain types of behavior or signs that may make us suspect the presence of this disease, because if it is diagnosed and treated early recovery is easier and faster.

Anorexia Nervosa consists of 2 subtypes:

  • restrictive: in the last 3 months it has not resorted to binge eating or purging. The low weight is due to food restriction or excessive exercise
  • Compulsive / purging: in the last 3 months has resorted to bingeing or purging.

If remission of any symptom occurs, specify:

  • In partial remission: after fulfilling all the criteria, the low weight is no longer met, but the fear to gain weight and the alteration of the perception of weight or silhouette is maintained.
  • In total remission: does not meet any criteria during a sustained period.

It should also be noted that they are usually given:

  • Other psychopathological alterations: Symptoms such as depressed mood, apathy, difficulty concentrating, anxiety, irritability, social isolation, loss of libido, sleep disturbances, obsessions and / or obsessive rituals are usually present. around the food. These alterations have a direct impact on the patient's daily life, greatly affecting the relationships he establishes with family and friends.
  • Physical alterations derived from severe malnutrition: Bone decalcification, heart problems, constant cold sensation, constipation, caigua of hair, etc.

Bulimia Nervosa

Bulimia Nervosa is a disorder of eating behavior that is characterized by episodes of binge eating (voracious and uncontrolled intake), in which a large amount of food is swallowed in a short space of time and usually in secret. Affected people try to compensate for the effects of overeating by self-induced vomiting and / or other purging maneuvers or increased physical activity.

They show an unhealthy worry about weight and figure, but there are not necessarily alterations in weight, since they can both have normal weight, as well as low weight or overweight. Bulimia nervosa is usually a hidden disorder, easily goes unnoticed, and is lived with feelings of shame and guilt. Hence, the affected person usually asks for help when the problem is already advanced. Bulimia Nervosa affects between 0.4% and 3% of the young Spanish population, especially girls.

As for binge eating, it should be noted that although the type of food consumed during this process varies, in most cases sweets and foods with a high calorie content are eaten. Binge eating can occur at any time of the day, but are more frequent after mid-afternoon and are usually triggered by altered mood states, interpersonal difficulties, intense hunger or feelings related to weight, body shape or food. They are accompanied by a feeling of lack of control and can reduce the discomfort transiently, but they are always followed by feelings of guilt, self-depreciation or depressed mood.

Eating Disorder BingeBinge

eating disorder is characterized by episodes of compulsive eating recurrent. It has many similarities with bulimia nervosa. The main difference is that the person suffering from a binge eating disorder does not perform compensatory behaviors (self-induced vomiting, abuse of laxatives and / or diuretics, fasting, excessive physical exercise, etc ...). It is also common for the person with this disorder to have depressive symptoms. One of the most common consequences of binge eating disorder is suffering from overweight or obesity, with the risks that this entails for health: diabetes, hypertension, cholesterol, etc.

Pica

Pica is a disorder that involves ingesting non-nutritive substances such as, for example, sand or plaster. It is most common during childhood, and in some cases it occurs in children who have autism or mental retardation.

The criteria to diagnose pica are:

Persistent ingestion of non-nutritive substances for a period of at least 1 month.

The ingestion of non-nutritive substances is inadequate for the level of development.

Ingestive behavior is not part of culturally sanctioned practices.

If the ingestive behavior appears exclusively in the course of another mental disorder (eg, mental retardation, pervasive developmental disorder, schizophrenia) it is of sufficient severity to deserve independent clinical attention.

Regurgitation

disorder This disorder is characterized by repeated regurgitation of food, which goes from the stomach to the mouth, to chew again. These repeated regurgitations make the maintenance of an adequate weight incompatible.

The criteria for diagnosing regurgitation disorder are:

Repeated regurgitation, without nausea or associated gastrointestinal disease, for at least one month, after a period of normal functioning.

Loss of weight or inability to reach normal weight.

Avoidance disorder / food restriction

This disorder involves a rejection of food and extremely capricious variations of what is normal eating behavior. It may be accompanied by regurgitation disorder.

Avoidance Disorder / Restriction of food intake

It is a eating disorder that is manifested by the denial and avoidance of food intake, sometimes due to characteristics such as color, smell or texture. As a result, the affected person has nutritional deficits that in some cases can put their lives at risk. Avoidance / Restriction of food intake is also more common in childhood, although it may arise and persist into adulthood.

Orthorexia

When there is an obsession with healthy eating, we are dealing with a case of orthorexia. Here, the person carries out an exhaustive and increasingly strict control of the components of the food they will consume.

In general, this begins when the person limits his / her diet a lot, for example avoiding the intake of red meat, eggs, sugars, dairy products and fats.

Vigorexia Vigorexia

is a disorder in which the person constantly worries about looking too small and weak. Therefore, those who suffer from this problem perform a lot of sports aimed at increasing muscle mass and for this, they also consume protein and anabolic supplements.

Disorder of the Unspecified Eating Conduct (TCANE)

The TCANE is that disorder in which there are symptoms related to eating behavior, but do not meet enough criteria to diagnose anorexia nervosa or bulimianervosa.For example, we talk about TCANE when a person has a restriction , a drop in weight, distortion of the body image, but no amenorrhea. Another case of TCANE would be that of a person who has binge eating and purging, but with a low and / or irregular frequency. The fact that the TCANE is an incomplete picture of anorexia or bulimia does not mean that it is less serious or that it does not need treatment.
